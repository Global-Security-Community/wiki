# Chapter Leadership Guide

Organizing local events is one of the most effective ways to build and energize your security community. In-person and virtual gatherings create meaningful connections, encourage collaboration, and foster a sense of belonging among members. Whether you're hosting educational sessions, workshops, or informal networking events, these activities drive lasting engagement and community growth.

While organizing an event might seem challenging initially, a structured approach and proper support make the process straightforward and rewarding. The more frequently you host events, the easier it becomes. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

## 1. Plan Your Event

- **Choose a topic or theme** – Consider hosting talks on security topics, hands-on workshops, panel discussions, or casual networking gatherings
- **Select a date and time** – Evening slots during weekdays often work well for local participation
- **Estimate attendance** – Start modestly for your first event (15–30 participants is a solid starting point)

## 2. Secure a Venue

- **Reach out to local organizations** – Tech companies, universities, libraries, and co-working spaces often welcome community events
- **Verify essential amenities** – Confirm availability of reliable internet, comfortable seating, accessible entrance, audio/video equipment, and facilities
- **Book in advance** – Aim to lock in your venue 6–8 weeks ahead, or sooner if possible

## 3. Identify Sponsors and Partners

- **Venue partners** – Some organizations will provide space at no cost in exchange for recognition or a brief presentation slot
- **Food and beverage supporters** – Reach out to local companies who may sponsor refreshments
- **Offer value** – Provide sponsors with logo placement, speaking opportunities, and visibility on your event materials

## 4. Create Your Event

- **Use your event platform** – Whether Meetup, Eventbrite, or Discord, create a dedicated event page that includes:
  - Clear, descriptive title
  - Detailed agenda or timeline
  - Speaker information and bios
  - Location, date, and time
  - Accessibility details and accommodations
  - RSVP deadline or registration instructions

- **Enable registration** – Once event details are finalized, open registration to your community

## 5. Promote Your Event

- **Leverage social media** – Share across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and other platforms where your audience gathers
- **Post in community spaces** – Share in relevant Discord channels, Slack groups, and mailing lists
- **Encourage word-of-mouth** – Ask current members to invite colleagues and peers
- **Use provided visuals** – Apply Global Security Community branding guidelines for consistent promotional materials

## 6. Engage During Your Event

- **Welcome attendees warmly** – Introduce yourself, explain your chapter's mission, and briefly describe the Global Security Community
- **Recognize contributors** – Publicly thank speakers, sponsors, and volunteers
- **Capture the moment** – Take photos and videos for later social sharing and documentation (refer to the [Event Photography Checklist](./event-photography-checklist.md) for guidance on what to capture)
- **Create networking opportunities** – Build in time for informal conversations before, during, and after the main agenda

## 7. Follow Up

- **Share event recap** – Post photos, key takeaways, speaker slides, and thank sponsors and speakers publicly
- **Gather feedback** – Send a brief survey to attendees to understand what worked well and what to improve
- **Maintain relationships** – Encourage attendees to join your chapter's communication channels and follow your updates
- **Document lessons learned** – Note what went well for future event planning
