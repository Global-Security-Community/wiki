# Community Branding Standards

![Global Security Community Logo](https://globalsecurity.community/assets/GlobalSecurityCommunityLogo.png)

These guidelines help you represent the Global Security Community consistently and professionally across all materials and platforms. Our brand identity reflects our commitment to accessibility, innovation in security, and building a welcoming global community.

The branding elements outlined here—from our logo and color palette to typography and visual language—work together to create a unified and recognizable brand presence. When you apply these standards consistently, you strengthen community recognition and maintain a professional image across all channels.

As a chapter leader, please review these guidelines carefully and apply them when creating promotional materials, social media graphics, presentations, and other content that represents our community.

## Logo Usage

Our Global Security Community logo is our most recognizable symbol and should be used consistently across all applications.

### Logo Guidelines

- **Maintain visibility** – Ensure the logo is clearly visible and not cluttered by surrounding elements
- **Preserve proportions** – Never stretch, compress, or alter the design
- **Provide clear space** – Maintain adequate surrounding space (at least equal to the logo's height)
- **Minimum size** – For digital use, maintain a minimum of 96 pixels in width; for print, at least 1 inch

### Logo Variations

Use the appropriate version based on your background:

- **Full color** – Use on light backgrounds
- **White version** – Use on dark backgrounds for contrast
- **Monochrome** – Use when color reproduction is limited or for special applications

### Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use the logo in its original proportions
- Maintain clear space around the logo
- Use appropriate color variations for your background
- Request guidance if unsure about usage

**DON'T:**
- Rotate, skew, or distort the logo
- Change colors or proportions
- Place the logo on backgrounds that reduce visibility
- Use outdated or unofficial versions

## Color Palette

Our carefully selected colors convey professionalism, trust, and innovation while maintaining excellent readability and accessibility.

### Primary Colors

- **Security Navy** (#0F3A66) – Primary blue for headers and main elements
- **Teal** (#1DB59A) – Secondary teal for accents and highlights
- **Security Orange** (#F39237) – Accent orange for emphasis and call-to-action
- **White** (#FFFFFF) – For clean backgrounds and contrast
- **Dark Gray** (#2D3E50) – For secondary text and borders

### Accent Colors

- **Success Green** (#059669) – For positive actions or confirmations
- **Alert Red** (#DC2626) – For warnings or important highlights
- **Information Blue** (#0EA5E9) – For informational content and links
- **Warning Amber** (#F59E0B) – For warnings and attention-seeking elements

### Color Usage

- Use Security Navy for headlines, body text, and primary UI elements
- Use Teal as the primary accent color for links and secondary elements
- Use Security Orange sparingly for important calls-to-action and highlights
- Maintain a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text
- Use semantic colors consistently (success green, alert red, info blue)

## Typography

Clear, accessible typography ensures our message reaches everyone effectively.

### Typefaces

- **Display/Headers** – Use a modern, clean sans-serif (e.g., similar to Segoe UI, Helvetica)
- **Body Text** – Use a readable sans-serif (e.g., Segoe UI, Roboto, or similar)

### Type Hierarchy

- **Headers (Heading 1)** – Large, bold, for page titles
- **Subheaders (Heading 2/3)** – Medium weight, for section divisions
- **Body Text** – Regular weight, 14–16px, for content readability
- **Small Text** – 12–14px, for captions or secondary information

### Best Practices

- Maintain strong visual hierarchy with size and weight variations
- Use consistent line spacing for readability
- Keep body text at a minimum of 14px for digital content
- Use color contrast to enhance readability

## Visual Language

Maintain a professional, approachable tone in all visual materials:

- **Photography** – Use high-quality photos of real community members and events
- **Illustrations** – Keep illustrations professional and on-brand
- **Icons** – Use simple, consistent icon sets across materials
- **Graphics** – Avoid cluttered designs; favor clean, modern layouts

## Social Media Guidelines

When creating content for social platforms:

- Always include the Global Security Community logo or mark
- Use brand colors to create visual consistency
- Maintain professional tone while staying approachable
- Include proper attribution and credit to speakers/partners
- Use consistent hashtags and branding in captions

## Questions and Support
