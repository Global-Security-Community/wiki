# Event Photography Checklist

Capturing compelling photos is essential to documenting and sharing the story of your events—from the energy of attendees to the expertise on stage and the meaningful moments in between. Use this checklist to ensure you capture a complete visual record that showcases the people, atmosphere, and community spirit of your event.

## Shots to Capture

1. **Registration and arrival** — Capture people collecting materials and entering the venue
2. **Venue setup** — Take a clean wide shot showing the stage, branding, seating, and overall space before the event starts
3. **Opening remarks or keynote** — Photograph the speaker in action with engaged audience members visible
4. **Group photo with all attendees** — Take this right after opening remarks when attendance is highest (consider a group selfie or formal gathering)
5. **Individual speaker moments** — Capture 4–5 different angles per speaker, ensuring the Global Security Community branding is visible when possible
6. **Audience engagement** — Photograph people taking notes, asking questions, smiling, or clapping
7. **Networking moments** — Capture attendees in conversation, introducing themselves, exchanging information, or building relationships
8. **Break time activities** — Photograph attendees enjoying refreshments, coffee breaks, or informal socializing
9. **Interactive sessions** — If your event includes workshops, demonstrations, or hands-on activities, document those in progress
10. **Branding and backdrops** — Photograph attendees near event banners, branded backdrops, or signage
11. **Speaker group photo** — Gather all speakers on stage for a group shot with clear event branding visible
12. **Organizer and volunteer moments** — Capture behind-the-scenes coordination and the efforts of those making the event happen
13. **Signage and decorations** — Take close-ups of logos, banners, sponsor recognition, and branding elements
14. **Natural moments and energy** — Capture candid smiles, laughter, and the genuine enthusiasm and energy of the event
15. **Closing moment** — Photograph the final remarks, group thank you, or closing applause

## Tips for Great Shots

- **Always include branding** — Feature the Global Security Community logo or event branding whenever possible to reinforce community identity
- **Vary your framing** — Mix wide shots (showing the full room), medium shots (showing groups), and close-ups (showing faces and expressions) to tell a complete visual story
- **Capture multiple formats** — Take both horizontal and vertical photos for flexibility across web, social media, and different platforms
- **Document key people** — Ensure every speaker, organizer, and important contributor appears in at least one quality photo
- **Focus on authenticity** — Capture genuine moments that reflect the real energy, connection, and excitement of your community gathering

The best event photos tell the story of your event—the people who attended, the purpose that brought them together, and the passion that drives your security community.

## After the Event

Once your event concludes, use your best photos for:

- **Social media posts** — Share highlights within 24-48 hours while the event is fresh
- **Chapter recap** — Include photos in your event debrief or wrap-up communications
- **Community website** — Add photos to your chapter's GitHub repository or website
- **Next event promotion** — Use compelling event photos to promote upcoming gatherings
- **Thank you messages** — Include photos when thanking speakers, sponsors, and organizers
